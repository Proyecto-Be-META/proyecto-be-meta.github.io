## Project overview

Be-META (*Beyond Metaheuristics*) is a research and innovation project that develops a reference framework for the visual modeling of problems and algorithms in the field of hospital management. Its goal is to improve the understanding, design and application of complex optimization methods through intuitive visual representations and interactive tools.

The project seeks to bring combinatorial optimization closer to professionals in the healthcare sector, facilitating decision-making in environments where efficiency, quality and time are critical factors. Through the creation of visual models and support systems, Be-META promotes a more intelligent and sustainable management of hospital resources.

In addition, it drives the transfer of knowledge between the university and the healthcare system, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and the training of researchers in key areas of engineering, computer science and health.

Be-META represents a commitment to applied innovation, where data science and optimization become tools to improve people's lives and strengthen the capacity of hospitals to respond to the challenges of the future.

## Project hypothesis

The main hypothesis of the Be-META project holds that the use of interactive visual models to represent optimization problems and algorithms can significantly improve the understanding, design and application of computational solutions in complex contexts such as hospital management. It starts from the idea that visualization facilitates communication between engineering experts and healthcare professionals, reducing the gap between mathematical theory and operational practice.

## Project objectives

1. **Design a visual modeling framework** that allows intuitive representation of optimization problems and the metaheuristic algorithms applied to the management of hospital resources.
2. **Develop functional prototypes** that integrate visualization with the simulation of hospital processes, facilitating decision-making in planning, staff allocation and patient management.
3. **Validate the effectiveness of the visual approach** through pilot studies in real or simulated environments, comparing the results with traditional optimization methods.
4. **Foster the transfer of knowledge** between the university and the healthcare system, creating spaces for interdisciplinary collaboration and training in applied computational thinking.
5. **Generate teaching materials and open resources** that allow the model to be replicated and extended to other fields of engineering and management.
