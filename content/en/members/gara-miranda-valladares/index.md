Gara Miranda Valladares holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Universidad de La Laguna (2009), where she defended her thesis "Algorithmic Skeletons for Combinatorial Optimization: Parallelizations and Applications". After her doctorate, she worked at the Science and Technology Park of Tenerife, promoting technology transfer and support for innovative companies. Since 2012, she has pursued her teaching and research career at the Universidad de La Laguna, where she is an Associate Professor (Profesora Titular) in the area of Languages and Computer Systems. Her research focuses on combinatorial optimization and the development of software to solve real-world problems using heuristics and metaheuristics. She has published more than 100 scientific works, including articles in high-impact journals and renowned international conferences. She has participated in multiple research projects and networks, collaborating on technology transfer through tools such as METCO, SCHOOLTHY, and RoblockLLy. She currently leads the research group on Parallel Languages and Algorithms and the Cultural Classroom of Computational Thinking.
