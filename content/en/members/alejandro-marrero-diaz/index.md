He obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from the Universidad de La Laguna (ULL) after completing a four-year FPI research fellowship awarded by the Government of the Canary Islands. His doctoral thesis focused on applying evolutionary computation to the automatic generation of optimization problem instances, contributing new methodologies to combinatorial optimization and computational intelligence for the analysis of problem structure and algorithm behavior. He received the Best Academic Record Award in the Master's in Computer Engineering (2018-2019) and the Extraordinary Doctorate Award (2024), both from the ULL.

He has been lead author of publications in high-impact journals (for example, Evolutionary Computation Journal, SoftwareX, Mathematics) and at relevant international conferences (for example, GECCO, PPSN), as well as in national forums (for example, CAEPIA, MAEB). In addition, he carried out a three-month research stay at the Edinburgh Parallel Computing Centre (EPCC) of the University of Edinburgh, a prestigious institution, funded by the European HPC-Europa3 project.
