Carlos Segura is a tenured researcher in the computing area of the Center for Research in Mathematics (CIMAT). He holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Universidad de La Laguna, completed in 2012. He specializes in applying optimization methods to practical problems across a variety of fields such as logistics, health, and telecommunications. He has published more than 100 articles, including more than 35 in top-tier journals. He is an international leader in optimization problems such as Job Shop Scheduling and graph partitioning, and has led teams that have won several international optimization competitions, such as the Wind Farm Layout Optimization Competition and the PACE Challenge 2024. He served as Technical Chair of the IEEE Congress on Evolutionary Computation and led the genetic algorithms track at the ACM Genetic and Evolutionary Computation Conference.
He is especially interested in competitive programming. He has trained several teams that qualified for the world finals of the International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) and received first prize in the coach category at the ICPC Challenge 2025 at the Latin American level. He has been an invited speaker at a variety of national and international events, including the ICPC Challenge Championship.
